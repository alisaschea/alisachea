2. Baseline Valuation (Nowak's Model)

This section presents the step-by-step valuation of Snap Inc. using Brian Nowak's base-case assumptions from Morgan Stanley. The analysis applies a WACC of 9.7% and a terminal growth rate of 3.5%.

Calculations

Step 1 - PV of FCF (2017-2025)

PV of FCF (2017-2025) = $3,775.76 million

Step 2 - PV of Terminal Value

TV  = FCF(N+1) / (WACC - g)
    = 4,196 / (0.097 - 0.035)
    = $67,677.42 million

PV of TV = 67,677.42 / (1 + 0.097)^9
         = $29,416.04 million

Step 3 - Enterprise Value

EV = PV of TV + PV of FCF
   = $29,416.04 + $3,775.76
   = $33,191.80 million

Step 4 - Total Equity Value

Equity = EV + Cash - Debt
       = $33,191.80 + $2,081 - $0
       = $35,272.80 million

Step 5 & 6 - Share Price

Share Price = $35,272.80 / 1,404 = $25.12

A higher WACC indicates a higher required rate of return, making the stock less attractive. Future cash flows are discounted more heavily, reducing their present value and the firm's valuation. A higher WACC also increases the cost of raising new capital, constraining growth opportunities.

A beta of 1.35 means Snap stock is 35% more volatile than the overall market. In a bull market Snap would outperform; in a bear market it would suffer larger losses. This beta is reasonable given Snap's unproven business model, untested management, slowing growth, and fierce competition from Facebook and Instagram.
